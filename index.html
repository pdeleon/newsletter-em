Speeding Through Gains

Ford (F -1.14%) kicked off August in style.

The company was up 5% on Monday before taking slight losses on Tuesday.

But July is what the company is most excited about:

  • July was Ford’s best month since 2008.
  • The automaker gained 32% in July.
  • Ford raised its dividend back to pre pandemic levels ($0.15 per share).

What drove Ford? The stock’s performance was driven by innovative product announcements and a solid second quarter earnings report.

Tis’ The Season For Earnings

Uber (UBER -18.90%) reported second-quarter losses yesterday, but made up for it in revenue.

  • Losses: $1.33 per share.
  • Revenue: $8.07 billion, much higher than the $7.39 billion estimate.

Uber had a boost in business for both its transportation and delivery business.

  • Travel: $13.4 billion, a 57% increase year over year.
  • Deliveries: $13.9 billion, a 12% increase year over year.

While U.S. store sales flourished during Q2, its performance in China, its second biggest market, saw a 44% decline in sales.

Despite the trouble in China, the coffee chain opened 318 new locations around the world last quarter, raising its global location count to 34,948.

Starbucks (SBUX -1.41%) also reported mixed earnings yesterday.

  • Earnings: Gained 84 cents per share.
  • Revenue: $8.15 billion, higher than the $8.11 billion expected.

Try Not to Worry

Batten down the hatches!

Americans are bracing for a recession.

The survey says: 66% of Americans worry that a major recession is around the corner.

  • Last year at this time; only 48% of Americans felt that way.

The main reason people are scared about a recession is high inflation. As prices increase, Americans take a pay cut because virtually everything becomes more expensive.

71% said their wages are not keeping pace with rising expenses.

General tips: You could ramp up your savings and cut back on spending - those are typically good ways to limit the impact of an economic downturn.

Think outside the box; find a side hustle, invest in alternatives, drink less Starbucks!

Workers Unwanted

June’s job openings were at the lowest level since September.

  • Total vacancies dropped to 10.7 million, a 605,000 decline from the last reading.
  • There are still about 1.8 job openings per available worker right now, for a total of 4.8 million positions.

Hiring also slowed down by 2% to 6.37 million during the month, with the unemployment rate hanging tight at 3.6%.
